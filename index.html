SEMantics

a structural equation modelling applications discussion group

SEMantics is a discussion group and seminar series about all things structural equation modelling (SEM). We meet monthly to discuss:

  • SEM applications for answering hypotheses in psychology, psychiatry, social science and epidemiology
  • SEM methodological advantages and challenges
  • Recent developments, innovations, and new techniques
  • Best practice (and cautionary tales)

SEM is a powerful technique for answering complex research questions involving multivariable, multiple regression equations. This might include questions about mediation, moderation, identifying unobserved latent classes in our data, and exploring longitudinal relationships. These types of questions can be answered within the SEM framework â€” offering a flexible and powerful framework for understanding our data. Once mostly the preserve of the Mplus program, options such as lavaan in R and gsem in Stata are increasingly making us spoilt for software choice.

SEMantics brings together like-minded researchers using SEM to answer research questions of interest. The group aims to share knowledge, provide peer support, and enable networking on topics related to SEM. Attendees should have at least a basic understanding and preferably some experience of using SEM. Over time, we hope for all attendees to deliver an informal presentation, which could be about ongoing research, software, or discussion of a published paper.
